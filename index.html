Silence

Silently I hear, As noisy screams Fill my ears with words That I barely understand. Neither stands back, Both want truth, Both think are right, Neither truly is. Understanding now, As they fight To their death inside, Silently I watch.

Proud

Near a river An indomitable soul Drank secure, As an arrow glowed behind. And suddenly All the birds flew away, As the water Got dyed in red. The soul stopped drinking, And looking back, There was a man, And an arrow through his neck.

The Moon

The sun looked down, As a red figure rises, Proud and sure, Severe and high. And for a moment, As such moon glowed With rising might, The sun stood pensive.

Gris

In deep darkness My heart cries alone, For it is helpless in such a world He wasn’t meant to be part of. And while I cough Weak and whining, A hand grabs my cloth, And with warmth, finally, I can close my eyes.

The End

At the end of the world There was an angel, In front of the everlasting abyss, With its little servant. They were the last who stood, And for the last night Before the time would end, A last order gave: “Bring me now The purest souls To sing tonight One last time” And back it got A heart of stone Tough and heavy As the servant said: “All the souls are gone In the depth of Tartarus; Only this survived, as so Strong the world forged her to be” And so she sang.
